Regulatory Disclosure and Consumer Protections

Transparency is a critical component of the finance industry in Australia. Under regulations set by the Australian Securities and Investments Commission, brokers are legally required to disclose how they are paid. This includes outlining both the upfront commission paid when a loan settles and any trailing commissions received over the life of the loan. If a broker intends to charge you a fee directly, this must be disclosed before you proceed, ensuring there are no hidden surprises.

Furthermore, since January 2021, brokers have been bound by the Best Interests Duty. This legislation requires brokers to act in the best interests of the consumer and to prioritise the consumer's interests when providing credit assistance. This regulatory framework adds a layer of security for borrowers, ensuring that the recommendations provided are aligned with your financial needs rather than just the broker's potential earnings.

Hidden Costs and Value in Lending

While the broker's service may not cost you directly, understanding the costs associated with the loan itself is vital. A skilled mortgage broker Bendigo borrowers use will help you identify and avoid unnecessary costs from the lender, such as high application fees or ongoing account-keeping fees. They can also assist in managing Lenders Mortgage Insurance (LMI), which is often a significant expense for those with smaller deposits.

For eligible first home buyers, a broker can guide you toward the First Home Guarantee, which allows you to purchase a home with a deposit as low as 5 percent without paying LMI. Even though the price caps for this scheme are reviewed annually, the potential savings on insurance premiums can be substantial. In this context, the value provided by the broker in navigating these schemes often outweighs the cost of doing it alone, where you might miss out on these opportunities.

Exceptions to the Standard Model

It is important to recognise that while residential home loans are typically serviced without a borrower fee, there are exceptions. Complex lending scenarios, commercial loans, or specific financial services may incur a fee for the broker's time and specialised expertise. However, the strict disclosure rules mean you will always be informed of these costs before you commit.

Common questions

Do I have to pay a fee to use a broker? Most residential mortgage brokers in Australia do not charge borrowers a fee. They are typically paid commission by the lender, and any fees you might be charged must be disclosed to you upfront under ASIC rules.

How does the broker get paid if I don't pay them? Brokers are generally paid a commission by the lender once your loan settles. This includes an upfront payment and potentially a trailing commission over the life of the loan, all of which must be disclosed in your loan documents.

Does using a broker increase my interest rate? No, the interest rate you receive through a broker is typically the same rate you would get by going directly to the lender. Brokers have access to the same rate sheets but can help you find the lender offering the most competitive rate for your situation.
